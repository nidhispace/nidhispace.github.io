What Are Volatile Organic Compounds?

VOCs are carbon-based molecules with high vapour pressure at ambient temperatures. They partition readily into the gas phase and participate in atmospheric chemistry across scales from local air quality to global climate.

BVOCs: The Plant-Atmosphere Interface

Biogenic VOCs are synthesized primarily through the methylerythritol phosphate (MEP) pathway in chloroplasts. Emission rates are controlled by temperature and photosynthetically active radiation (PAR).

Why They Matter

  • Tropospheric O₃ formation: VOCs + NOₓ + hν → O₃ (ground-level smog)
  • Secondary Organic Aerosol (SOA): Oxidation products condense to particle phase
  • OH radical budget: VOCs are the dominant sink for atmospheric OH
  • Climate feedback: SOA alters cloud condensation nuclei (CCN) concentrations

How We Measure Them

  • GC-MS/FID: Gas chromatography with mass spectrometry — gold standard for speciation; requires pre-concentration on adsorbent traps.
  • PTR-MS: Proton-transfer-reaction mass spectrometry — real-time detection using H₃O⁺ reagent ions; no sample preparation needed.
  • FTIR / DOAS: Open-path optical methods for column-integrated and in-situ measurements.

📄 Featured Research

Impacts of Convection, Chemistry, and Forest Clearing on BVOCs over the Amazon

Nidhi Tripathi et al. • Nature Communications, 2025

DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-59953-2

🔬 Key Finding 1:
Deep convection redistributes BVOCs from the boundary layer to the upper troposphere (8–14 km), extending their atmospheric lifetime and influencing upper-level chemistry.
🌳 Key Finding 2:
Forest clearing reduces isoprene emissions by 40–60% locally, but the convective transport of remaining BVOCs partially offsets this loss at regional scales.
🛰️ Methodology:
Combined aircraft measurements (ATom campaign), satellite retrievals (TROPOMI HCHO), and GEOS-Chem modeling to disentangle emission vs. transport effects.
💡 Implication:
Amazon deforestation impacts atmospheric chemistry far beyond the local scale through altered convective transport pathways of BVOCs.

Oxidation Kinetics & Lifetimes

The atmospheric lifetime (τ) of a VOC is determined by its reactivity with the major oxidants:

$$\tau_{\text{VOC}} = \frac{1}{k_{\text{OH}}[\text{OH}] + k_{\text{O}_3}[\text{O}_3] + k_{\text{NO}_3}[\text{NO}_3]}$$

For most BVOCs, the OH reaction dominates daytime removal. [OH] ≈ 1×10⁶ molec cm⁻³ (24h global average). As shown in Tripathi et al. (2025), upper tropospheric OH concentrations can extend BVOC lifetimes by 2–3×.

Isoprene Oxidation Mechanism

The fate of isoprene peroxy radicals (ISOPO₂) critically depends on the NOₓ regime. Tripathi et al. (2025) show that over the Amazon, the transition between low-NOₓ and convective-influenced regimes determines SOA vs. O₃ production.

Tripathi et al. (2025) found that convection transports ISOPO₂ and ISOPOOH to the upper troposphere, where different chemistry prevails, altering the SOA/O₃ partitioning.

Isoprene Emission: Temperature Response

Isoprene emission follows the Guenther (G93) algorithm. Tripathi et al. (2025) applied this with MODIS land surface temperature to constrain Amazon emissions.

$$I = I_S \cdot C_T \cdot C_L \quad \text{where} \quad C_T = \frac{\exp\left(\frac{C_{T1}(T-T_S)}{RT_ST}\right)}{1 + \exp\left(\frac{C_{T2}(T-T_M)}{RT_ST}\right)}$$

Guenther et al. (1993, 2012). Tripathi et al. (2025) used TS = 303 K, CT1 = 95,000 J mol⁻¹, CT2 = 230,000 J mol⁻¹, TM = 314 K.

Convective Transport of BVOCs (Tripathi et al., 2025)

Deep convection over the Amazon rapidly lifts BVOCs from the boundary layer (0–2 km) to the upper troposphere (8–14 km), fundamentally altering their chemical fate.

Key Results:
  • Vertical redistribution: Isoprene concentrations of 0.5–2 ppb observed at 10–14 km altitude during convective events
  • Lifetime extension: BVOC lifetime increases 2–5× in the upper troposphere due to lower OH and temperature
  • Long-range transport: Convected BVOCs can be transported 2000–4000 km downwind before oxidation
  • Forest clearing effect: Deforestation reduces boundary layer emissions but also weakens convection, creating complex regional feedbacks
