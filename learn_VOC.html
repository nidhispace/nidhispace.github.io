What Are Volatile Organic Compounds?

VOCs are carbon-based molecules with high vapour pressure at ambient temperatures, enabling rapid partitioning into the gas phase. They span a wide range of functional groups including alkanes, alkenes, aromatics, and oxygenates.

BVOCs: The Plant-Atmosphere Interface

Biogenic VOCs are synthesized through metabolic pathways in vegetation, primarily via the methylerythritol phosphate (MEP) pathway for isoprene and monoterpenes. Emission rates are functions of temperature and photosynthetically active radiation (PAR).

Why They Matter: Atmospheric Impacts

  • Tropospheric O₃ formation: VOCs + NOₓ + hν → O₃
  • Secondary Organic Aerosol (SOA): Oxidation products partition to particle phase
  • OH radical budget: VOCs are the primary sink for atmospheric OH
  • Climate feedback: SOA affects cloud condensation nuclei (CCN)

Measurement Principles

Modern trace gas analysis employs several complementary techniques:

  • GC-MS/FID: Gold standard for speciation; requires pre-concentration
  • PTR-MS: Real-time, soft chemical ionization with H₃O⁺ reagent ions
  • FTIR/DOAS: Open-path optical methods for column-integrated measurements

Oxidation Kinetics

The atmospheric lifetime of VOCs is governed by reactions with OH, NO₃, and O₃. The OH reaction dominates daytime removal:

$$\tau_{\text{VOC}} = \frac{1}{k_{\text{OH}}[\text{OH}] + k_{\text{O}_3}[\text{O}_3] + k_{\text{NO}_3}[\text{NO}_3]}$$
