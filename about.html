1871
19th Century · Empire

German Empire Established

After the victory in the Franco-Prussian War, the German Empire was officially proclaimed on January 18, 1871, in the Hall of Mirrors at the Palace of Versailles. King Wilhelm I was crowned the first German Emperor, uniting numerous independent states under Prussian leadership and triggering an era of rapid industrial growth and global influence.

1914 – 1918
20th Century · Conflict

World War I

Germany was a leading member of the Central Powers during World War I, a conflict that reshaped Europe's political landscape. Immense social and economic hardship followed four years of brutal fighting. The abdication of Kaiser Wilhelm II in 1918 ended the monarchy and gave birth to the fragile Weimar Republic.

1933
20th Century · Dark Chapter

Nazi Party Rises to Power

In January 1933, Adolf Hitler was appointed Chancellor of Germany. The new regime swiftly centralised authority, curtailed political opposition, and established a highly controlled state — marked by militarisation, propaganda, and policies that ultimately triggered World War II and the Holocaust, an unparalleled human tragedy.

1945
20th Century · End of War

End of World War II

World War II ended in Europe with Germany's unconditional surrender in May 1945. The country lay in ruins. Germany was divided into Allied occupation zones, eventually creating two states: the Federal Republic (West Germany) and the Democratic Republic (East Germany), shaping Cold War politics for decades.

1990
20th Century · Reunification

German Reunification

After the fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989, East and West Germany formally reunified on October 3, 1990. This historic event ended the Cold War division, restoring Germany as a single sovereign nation. The reunification brought profound political, social, and economic challenges — and stood as a powerful symbol of freedom and unity in Europe.

Today
21st Century · Modern State

Modern Germany

Today, Germany is a federal parliamentary republic and Europe's largest economy. It plays a crucial role in the European Union and global affairs, renowned for advanced industries, cultural heritage, and its commitment to democracy, human rights, and environmental sustainability. Its rich history continues to shape a vibrant, diverse society.
